\subsection{RQ1: Temporal Hazard Quality and Actionable Timing}
\label{subsec:rq1_results}

RQ1 asks whether the temporal model is adequate for downstream policy and subgroup evaluation. The empirical answer depends on weekly hazard discrimination and calibration quality over operationally relevant risk ranges.

\paragraph*{Discrimination.}
Under the enrollment-level stratified temporal split, row-level weekly hazard discrimination is
\begin{equation*}
AUC_{train}=0.8350, \qquad AUC_{test}=0.8405.
\end{equation*}
The small train--test gap indicates stable performance under temporal holdout and supports the use of the fitted hazard model as the backbone for subsequent simulated-regime analysis.

\subsection{RQ2: Policy Simulation and Structural Regime Contrast}
\label{subsec:rq2_results}

RQ2 asks whether an explicit policy rule changes model-implied survival trajectories under fixed protocol settings. The framework yields interpretable structural contrasts, but the sign and magnitude depend strongly on how the policy enters the system.

\paragraph*{Definition used in reporting.}
\begin{equation*}
\Delta S(t)=\bar S^{(1)}(t)-\bar S^{(0)}(t),
\qquad
\bar S^{(a)}(t)=\frac{1}{N}\sum_i \hat S_i^{(a)}(t).
\end{equation*}
Positive $\Delta S(t)$ means higher survival under the policy regime than under baseline.

\paragraph*{Figure-level evidence for RQ2.}
Figure~\ref{fig:deltas_rq2} is the primary figure for RQ2. The key readout is the sign over time and the horizon-specific magnitude by scenario.

\paragraph*{Mechanism-aware diagnostics (reported run).}
The exported operator specification (\texttt{table\_policy\_mech\_operator\_spec.csv}) records a click-based, stateful channel mode (\texttt{clicks\_plus\_stateful\_recency\_streak}) with $W=2$, $\alpha_{week0}=0.35$, and $\alpha_{week1}=0.10$. In the same run, overwrite diagnostics report changes concentrated in click-based rows (4{,}816 of 232{,}417 rows; 2.07\%) and no non-active propagation rows in the compact overwrite summary. Hazard-delta diagnostics on changed rows indicate opposite mean shifts by branch (mechanism-aware: $+0.000207$; shock: $-0.001719$), which is consistent with the observed negative $\Delta S_{mech}$ under the frozen shared schedule.

\paragraph*{Sensitivity scope.}
The RQ2 grid sweep (
\texttt{table\_rq2\_sensitivity\_grid.csv}, 216 configurations) varies trigger and schedule parameters ($r^*$, $W$, decay rule, $\alpha_{week0}$, $\alpha_{week1}$, and scenario intensity). In the exported package, this sweep does not add feature-channel variants beyond the click-based mechanism-aware operator.

Under the updated protocol, mechanism-aware interpretation is conditioned on the mechanism-validity gate and fragility diagnostics (\texttt{table\_policy\_mech\_validity\_gate.csv}, \texttt{table\_policy\_mech\_channel\_attribution.csv}, \texttt{table\_policy\_mech\_fragility\_by\_scenario.csv}), which are reported as audit controls rather than as causal-identification evidence.

\paragraph*{RQ2 conclusion.}
For RQ2, the main result is that the framework compares explicit regimes on a common temporal backbone and produces interpretable survival contrasts at fixed horizons. In the present configuration, positive contrasts are restricted to the shock branch and are highly scenario-dependent, while the mechanism-aware branch remains weak to negative under the current shared schedule.

\subsection{RQ3: Subgroup Validation via Change-in-Gap}
\label{subsec:rq3_results}

RQ3 tests whether the same policy regime produces differential group contrasts, reported with uncertainty.

\paragraph*{Reported quantity.}
\begin{equation*}
\Delta Gap(T)=Gap^{(1)}(T)-Gap^{(0)}(T).
\end{equation*}
Negative values indicate a reduction in the reported gender gap (F minus M) under policy relative to baseline.

\subsection{Results Synthesis}
\label{subsec:results_synthesis}

The empirical evidence supports the paper objective in four direct points:

\begin{enumerate}
\item Weekly hazard quality is sufficient for temporal risk ranking and trajectory analysis, with caution in the highest-risk tail bins (RQ1).
\item Regime comparison is operational and auditable through $\Delta S(t)$, but positive gains are scenario-dependent and do not extend to the current mechanism-aware branch (RQ2).
\item The same policy can be audited for subgroup differentials via $\Delta Gap(T)$, with directionally stable but very small contrasts in the reported gender example (RQ3).
\item The main interpretation remains directionally coherent under benchmark and robustness axes.
\end{enumerate}

These are framework-validation claims under explicit scenario rules, not causal claims about real interventions in OULAD.

\subsection{Interpretation Boundaries}

The results are interpreted under three limits:
\begin{itemize}
\item policy and fairness outputs are simulated regime contrasts;
\item horizon-validity constraints under censoring are binding for metric interpretation;
\item endpoint and domain-shift robustness indicate sensitivity ranges, not universal transportability.
\end{itemize}

Within these limits, the section answers the paper objective directly: a reproducible temporal pipeline can translate weekly risk into structured policy and subgroup diagnostics.
